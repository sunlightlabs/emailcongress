What is this service?

Members of Congress donâ€™t have official emails for the public to use, but they do have forms. This service makes navigating their complex contact forms as easy as emailing them. It allows you to contact them via email after you fill out your information just once.

How do I use this service?

Get started simply by sending a message to your representatives to myreps@emailcongress.us.

You'll get a confirmation email with a link to sign up. Once you're signed up, we'll send off your first message to your representatives. From then on, you'll be able to continue writing to them by email instead of going through their contact forms.

Is there another way to contact Congress?

Yes! You can call, fax or mail a message and show up to their offices in person to discuss issues that are important to you. Look up how to contact them on usa.gov.

Why can I only select the three titles: Mr., Mrs. and Ms.?

Many congressional webforms require a title, but there is no standard list across offices. Some include honorifics from medical, military and other professional fields, but the three options we provide are the minimum selection that spans all webforms.

Why isn't my message being sent?

Message failures can occur for many reasons, some outside of your or our control.

Messages can fail when there are issues on the congressional webforms we're passing your message through, or our system may need to be updated to reflect changes.

Sometimes errors in the information from the message can cause issues, such as a lengthy message that exceeds the limit on webforms, which is set individually be each congressional office.

If the address you entered does not fall in the congressional district you're trying to send your message to (or is not recognized by the congressional web form), it can result in an unsent message.

Who made this?

The Sunlight Foundation, a nonpartisan nonprofit dedicated to government transparency and accountability, built this project to help connect citizens to their representatives in Congress. We're not affiliated with government.

The open source code for this project is available on GitHub.
